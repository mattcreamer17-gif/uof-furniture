The frame

Put each layer where it belongs.

Most businesses looking at AI put it in the wrong place. Roughly 60% of what feels like it needs AI is ordinary code and database work. Another 30% is rule-based logic: if the config has three tall partitions and an acoustic pod, the line-item quote lands within these bounds. Only the last 10% is genuinely AI work.

For UOF specifically, that 60/30/10 reads like this.

  • 60

    Code and database

    The customer intake form, the Bralco spec lookup, the financing paperwork.

  • 30

    Rule-based logic

    Quote rules, approval routing, what triggers a designer review.

  • 10

    Genuine AI

    First-pass space plan, post-install summary, translation of Italian product copy into client-ready specs.

Put the layers in the right order and a four-week quote cycle becomes a four-day one, without Marco leaving the design calls.
